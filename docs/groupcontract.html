Group Contract

Expectations

  • For group meetings members are expected to arrive at least 5 minutes beforehand and have completed all work that was agreed upon in the previous meetings if no extensions have been discussed.
  • Members are expected to have their webcams and microphones turned on to increase the effectiveness of communication and member relationships.
  • Members will be expected to attend at least one of the weekly meetings at the decided upon time as well as the scheduled tutorial that each member is enrolled in. These meetings will amount to approximately 3-4 hours a week of planning and communication about the current state of members workflow.
  • Members are expected to have the current progress of their work updated on Trello prior to the meeting and it be an accurate representation of the current status of their assigned work.
  • Members are expected to have the files on the Github repository up to date prior to the start of each meeting.
  • Members are expected to contribute to and volunteer for work if needed, waiting to be assigned work by others and not offering help in areas that are needed is not acceptable.
  • Members are required to not miss 2 weekly meetings in a row.
  • If Any of these expectations are breached further action may be taken following the guidelines laid out below in unmet expectations.

Unmet Expectations

If members are having trouble finishing any assigned work they are expected to notify the group at least 5 days before the decided upon due date and let the other group members know so alternative arrangements can be made. Any problems that arise will initially be attempted to be dealt with by our group members, those involved with the issue will need to be present and at least one other group member will need to be present for mediation if necessary. If a solution cannot be reached or group members are not in attendance to the resolution session the issue will be escalated to our tutor and or course coordinator. Examples of problems that could arise include but are not limited to unfinished work, disagreements on quantity or quality of work, group participation and repeated requests for work extension beyond decided due date.

Peer Feedback

An anonymous document that members can use to offer feedback will be used at the end of each assignment submission for constructive feedback and criticism only. Each member will then be given their relevant feedback for reflection. Members agree to being given feedback throughout the duration of the assignment if other group members feel that it is needed, this can be brought up at weekly meetings or through communication channels such as Microsoft Teams, any instances of this should also be recorded in feedback given at the end of each assignment.

Responses

Members are expected to respond to requests on enquiries made on Microsoft Teams within 24 hours.

Tools

Use of Trello for keeping track of current tasks will be used and members are expected to update the status of their assignments using the Trello cards when appropriate, the Trello board has been shared and confirmed with each group member. Prior to the beginning of each of the weekly meetings members are expected to have their Trello assignment cards up to date and be accurate to the current progress of their assigned work. Meetings will be held using Microsoft Teams at predetermined times agreed upon by all members, these meetings will be recorded and archived for any further reference if needed. Teams will also be used for scheduling meetings using the calendar. Trello will be linked to the our group teams page for easy access and convenience. Github will be used by all members for the back up and sharing of our documents, the Github repository has already been created and shared and confirmed with each member with each member. Prior to each weekly meeting members are expected to have their documentation and files up to date on Github for accurate and up to date file sharing.
